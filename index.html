Hitter's Glossary
Color Code
Green — Favorable for hitter
Elite production, strong contact quality, or favorable matchup.
Amber — Average / Playable
Near league average. Context determines value.
Red — Unfavorable
Below average or high-risk metrics. Downgrade vs field.
Production (Results)
wOBA
Weighted On-Base Average. Weights each outcome by run value. League avg ~.320. Better than OPS for true offensive value.
wRC+
Weighted Runs Created Plus. Park and league adjusted. 100 = exactly league avg. 150 = 50% better than avg.
ISO
Isolated Power (SLG minus AVG). Pure extra-base hit rate. Elite: .250+. Average: .150.
Contact Quality (True Talent)
xwOBA
Expected wOBA from EV, launch angle, sprint speed. Removes luck. Key indicator of real vs fluky production.
Barrel%
Optimal EV + launch angle. Strongest HR predictor. Elite: 15%+. Average: 7%.
HardHit%
Exit velocity 95+ mph. Elite: 50%+. Average: 38%.
Avg EV
Average exit velocity on contact. Elite: 93+ mph. League avg: ~88 mph.
Plate Discipline
K%
Strikeout rate per PA. League avg ~22%. Below 18% = elite contact. Above 30% = significant K risk.
BB%
Walk rate per PA. League avg ~8.5%. Above 15% = elite eye.
SwStr%
Swing-and-miss rate. Below 8% = elite contact. Above 14% = K-prone.
O-Swing%
Chase rate outside zone. Below 25% = disciplined. Above 35% = exploitable.
Swing Mechanics
Attack Angle
Degrees of upswing at contact. 5–20° is the ideal lift window. Higher = more vulnerability to flat deliveries.
Bat Speed
Average mph at sweet spot. 75+ mph = fast swing. Top tier: 78+ mph.
Squared-Up%
Efficiency converting bat speed to exit velocity. Higher = better contact efficiency.
Blast%
Fast swing + squared up combined. Best bat tracking power indicator.
Matchup Badges
PMS 8–10
Strong structural advantage over today's pitcher. Prioritize in DFS lineups.
PMS 5–7
Neutral matchup. Season stats and salary efficiency matter more here.
PMS 1–4
Structural disadvantage. Consider fading or downgrading. See PMS tab for details.
HES
Hitter Environment Score — grades how favorable today's park and conditions are for offense. Applies to the entire lineup, not individual hitters.
Pillar 1 — Park Factors (40%)
HR Factor
FanGraphs park-adjusted HR rate. Coors (1.35), Yankee Stadium (1.18), Petco Park (0.84). The biggest swing in HES.
wOBAcon Factor
How much the park amplifies contact results — driven by wall distances, foul territory, and playing surface.
BB/K Factor
Parks with poor sight lines or small foul zones subtly affect plate discipline outcomes.
Pillar 2 — Dimensions (25%)
Wall Distances
LF, CF, RF distances. Sub-330 LF or RF = elevated HR ceiling. 400+ CF = significant HR suppression.
Wall Heights
The Green Monster (37 ft) kills HRs but inflates doubles. Low fences = more HR upside.
Altitude
Coors Field (5,200 ft) is the biggest outlier — thin air adds ~30 ft of carry to fly balls. Other parks negligible.
Pillar 3 — Weather (35%)
Temperature
Ball carries further in heat. 85°F+ meaningfully boosts offense. Below 50°F suppresses it. Domes lock this at 72°F effective neutral.
Wind Direction
Out to CF = maximum HR boost. Out to LF/RF = partial boost. In = HR suppressor. Crosswind = neutral. Domes score 0 on this pillar.
Wind Speed
10+ mph out = significant impact. 15+ mph = one of the highest-leverage DFS factors of the day. Under 5 mph = negligible.

PMS
Pitcher Matchup Score — a per-batter 1–10 grade of how well this hitter's profile exploits today's starting pitcher. Select a pitcher first to see live PMS badges on every row.
Component 1 — Platoon Splits (25%)
Platoon advantage
LHH vs RHP or RHH vs LHP gains structural advantage. The hitter's own platoon split wOBA is weighted against the pitcher's allowed split wOBA to quantify the edge.
Component 2 — Pitcher Stuff Quality (20%)
Stuff+ / Pitching+
A pitcher with below-average stuff (Stuff+ < 90) gives away more PMS points to the opposing hitters. Elite stuff (120+) caps upside even for high-contact hitters.
Component 3 — Swing Plane vs Arm Angle (25%)
Attack angle vs VAA
High attack angle hitters (20°+) struggle against low-slot / sidearm pitchers whose flat vertical approach angle (VAA near 0°) can't be elevated. This mismatch suppresses PMS even when platoon is favorable.
Optimal match
Moderate attack angle (8–15°) vs high arm slot pitchers = ideal swing-plane alignment. Hitter can elevate without overswinging.
Component 4 — Hitter Contact Quality (20%)
Barrel% + HardHit% + xwOBA
A high-contact-quality hitter punishes poor pitchers harder. This component ensures that even in a neutral matchup, elite batters score higher than weak ones facing the same arm.
Component 5 — Recent Form (10%)
L7 / L14 wOBA
A hot hitter picks up bonus points. A cold hitter (L14 wOBA < .250) loses PMS points even if the structural matchup is strong. Form is a tiebreaker, not a primary driver.

Stack Grade
A single letter grade (A+ to F) summarizing how aggressively to stack this lineup today. Combines individual pitcher matchup quality with the ballpark environment.
Formula
60% — PMS Average
Average PMS score across all 9 hitters, with extra weight on batting spots 1–4. A lineup with elite top-of-order matchups grades higher than one where only the bottom third benefits.
40% — HES Score
The ballpark environment score. A great pitcher matchup in a dome on a cold night is worth less than the same matchup at Coors on a windy summer evening.
Elite Bonus
If 3+ hitters in spots 1–4 score PMS 8+, a bonus lifts the grade. Top-of-order concentration is where DFS stacks score — this rewards it explicitly.
Grade Scale
A+ / A
Elite lineup-wide matchup + favorable park. Target 3–4 hitters. This is the lineup to build around in GPPs.
B+ / B
Above average across the board. A 2–3 man stack is justified. Look for top-order concentration and avoid the bottom third unless salary forces it.
C+ / C
Mixed signals — possibly a weak pitcher but a suppressed park, or good park but a tough arm. Salary savings may be the primary draw. Use sparingly in GPPs.
D / F
Fade this lineup entirely. Tough pitcher + hostile environment. Only use individual hitters at extreme salary discounts for cash games.
Important
Stack Grade doesn't account for salary. A B+ grade at $9K/hitter may be better value than an A grade at $5K/hitter. Always cross-reference with DraftKings salary to find the optimal exposure level.
Pitcher's Glossary
Color Code
Green — Favorable for pitcher
Strong process metrics, good command, sustainable results.
Amber — Average / Watch
Near league average or trending worth monitoring.

The + Metrics (100 = League Avg)
Stuff+
Raw pitch nastiness: velocity, movement, spin, release. Stabilizes after ~80 pitches.
Location+
Command quality adjusted for count and pitch type. Takes ~400 pitches to stabilize.
Pitching+
Combines Stuff+ and Location+ with batter handedness. Most complete single pitch quality metric.
Arm Angle & Physics
Arm Angle
0° = sidearm, 90° = over the top. Lower = flatter plane = harder for steep swingers to lift.
VAA (Vertical Approach Angle)
Angle pitch crosses the plate. Closer to 0° = flatter. Fastballs typically -4° to -5°.
IVB (Induced Vertical Break)
Ride beyond gravity. High IVB + low release = deceptive carry.
RV/100
Run Value per 100 pitches. Positive = pitcher wins that pitch type.
Process / True Talent
xFIP
Expected FIP with HR normalized to league avg. Best single ERA estimator.
SIERA
Adds batted ball profile to FIP. Most complete process ERA estimator.
SwStr%
Swing-and-miss rate. Best in-game K predictor. League avg ~11.2%.
CSW%
Called Strike + Whiff Rate. Fuller picture of pitch effectiveness.
BABIP / LOB%
Luck indicators. BABIP lg avg ~.300. LOB% lg avg ~72%. Extremes regress.
Batted Ball
Barrel%
Optimal EV + launch angle allowed. Best HR predictor against this pitcher.
HardHit%
Statcast EV 95+ mph allowed.
GB% / FB% / LD%
High GB% suppresses HRs. High FB% + Hard% = HR risk. LD% drives BABIP.
